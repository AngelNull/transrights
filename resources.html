Petitions

These petitions only allow UK residents to sign them, if you are outside of the UK, make them known on social media instead of signing them yourself.

The UK government is required to respond to all petitions made on the parliament website when they reach 10,000 signatures.

When a petition reaches 100,000 signatures, parliament considers debating the topic.
This means that critical change can be pressured out of the government, not allowing them to stay silent on issues that are truly important, although petitions do not mean change. Other ways of pressuring the government such as protesting is still as important as petitioning.

  • Display LGBT+ flags without council permission - Currently, any pride flag other than the rainbow pride flag may not be displayed outside a building without having to get planning permission as it is treated as advertising. This can prevent LGBT+ people from displaying pride in their specific identities in their own communities.
  • Update gender identity guidance for schools to support students  - Updated guidance is needed to help schools support their trans and gender-questioning students. This should encourage teachers to ask students for their pronouns and preferred names.

Writing to MPs

Writing to your MP is a really important part of making your voice heard, when many people reach out to their MP, they will feel more pressed to do something.

  • Kite Trust Guide - This site contains multiple template letters which can be sent to your local MP as well as key ministers.
  • Stonewall Guide - This site contains more in-depth information on how to make the letter you're sending personal to you, not just a template.
  • MermaidsUK Guide - This site is the simplified version of both guides, providing a template and also showing steps on how to send it and make sure it goes to the right place.
